IndiGo Airlines — Fleet Utilization & Safety Performance Review (FY 2024-25)
=============================================================================

Compiled by: Chief Operations Office & Continuing Airworthiness Management, IndiGo Airlines
Review Period: April 2024 – March 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

Aircraft Utilization Summary
------------------------------

The IndiGo fleet averaged 11.2 block hours per aircraft per day during FY 2024-25, ranking
among the highest utilization rates for low-cost carriers in the Asia-Pacific region. The
A320 sub-fleet (including AC001/VT-INA, AC010/VT-INJ, and AC011/VT-INK) averaged 11.8
hours daily, driven by high-frequency trunk routes such as the DEL-BOM corridor (flight
6E-201, route R001) and the BLR-HYD sector (flight 6E-205, route R005).

Aircraft AC001 (VT-INA, A320neo) recorded the highest individual utilization at 4,287
block hours across 2,314 cycles for the year. Its primary assignment on the DEL-BOM route
meant an average of six daily sectors, with Capt. Anil Kapoor (CR001) commanding 22% of
those sectors. Despite heavy utilization, AC001 achieved a 99.6% dispatch reliability rate,
with only two technical delays — both attributed to minor APU faults resolved within
30 minutes by the DEL line maintenance team.

Aircraft AC003 (VT-INC, A321neo) logged 3,890 block hours. Following the Pratt & Whitney
hot section inspection at MRO-SIN (maintenance record M003) in January 2024, the engine
performed within nominal parameters for the remainder of the year. However, a second
unscheduled engine inspection was triggered in November 2024 after an in-flight engine
vibration alert on flight F009 (DEL-DXB route R009). The aircraft was ferried to MRO-SIN
and returned to service after 18 days, with a fan blade erosion finding documented under
maintenance record M021. Cost impact: INR 82,00,000.

ATR Fleet Regional Operations
-------------------------------

The ATR 72-600 sub-fleet (AC015/VT-INO, AC016/VT-INP, AC017/VT-INQ) operated 14,200
revenue sectors across 38 regional routes during the year. Average daily utilization was
8.4 block hours per aircraft — lower than the A320 fleet due to shorter sector lengths and
limited night operations at regional airports.

Aircraft AC015 (VT-INO) completed its scheduled A-Check (M005) at IndiGo Hangar-HYD on
April 14, 2024 as planned. The inspection confirmed the brake pad replacement performed
in January had resolved the wear issue. A subsequent B-Check in October 2024 (M018) at
the same facility revealed a propeller blade surface crack on the #1 engine, requiring blade
replacement under warranty from the PW127M manufacturer. Technician TEC007 led the repair,
which was completed in 5 days with a cost of INR 12,50,000.

New inductee AC017 (VT-INQ), delivered in August 2024, was assigned to the HYD-VTZ-MAA
triangle serving Vizag and Chennai. First Officer Priya Nair (CR002, EMP10002) flew 32 of
AC017's initial revenue sectors as part of her route familiarization ahead of the command
upgrade evaluation.

Safety Performance Indicators
-------------------------------

IndiGo recorded 58 safety incidents across the fleet in FY 2024-25, down from 71 in the
prior year — a 18.3% improvement. The incident rate declined to 0.42 per 1,000 flight hours
from 0.52, maintaining the airline well below the industry benchmark of 1.2.

Category breakdown:
  - ATC / Ground delays: 22 incidents (37.9%) — DEL remained the primary contributor,
    consistent with the pattern established in I001 (January 2024). The proposed schedule
    adjustment for flight 6E-201 (shifting departure by 15 minutes) was implemented in
    October 2024 and reduced morning slot conflicts at DEL by 40%.

  - Bird / Wildlife strikes: 9 incidents (15.5%) — The January 2024 bird strike on AC005
    (incident I003) was the only event requiring more than overnight grounding. A new
    bird scaring system was installed at BLR Kempegowda runway 09R in June 2024, reducing
    strike events at BLR by 60% in H2 compared to H1.

  - Technical / Mechanical: 14 incidents (24.1%) — The hydraulic leak on AC010 (I005) and
    the fan blade erosion on AC003 (M021) were the two most significant events. AC010
    underwent a full hydraulic system overhaul during its C-Check in September 2024 (M019)
    at Air India Engineering DEL, which replaced all flexible hydraulic hoses and connectors
    fleet-wide as a preventive measure. Cost: INR 1,20,00,000 across the A320 fleet.

  - Weather-related diversions: 8 incidents (13.8%) — Concentrated in the July–September
    monsoon season, primarily affecting arrivals into BOM (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj
    International Airport) and GOI (Dabolim Airport / Manohar International Airport).

  - Cabin safety events: 5 incidents (8.6%) — Including one turbulence injury on the
    DEL-DXB route in December 2024. Senior cabin crew Rahul Verma (CR006, EMP10006)
    was commended for rapid first-aid response; the passenger was transferred to Dubai
    medical services upon landing.

Passenger Impact & Service Recovery
--------------------------------------

Passenger Aarav Sharma (P001, Platinum tier, FF100001) was affected by the AC003 schedule
disruption in November 2024 (M021 engine event). His DEL-DXB booking on flight F009 was
re-accommodated onto a partner airline within 4 hours. As a Platinum frequent flyer, he
received a complimentary Business Plus upgrade on his return sector and 5,000 bonus loyalty
points. His post-event survey rated the recovery as "Excellent."

Passenger Diya Patel (P002, Gold tier) experienced a second baggage irregularity in August
2024 on the BLR-BOM route. Root cause analysis traced the issue to a barcode scanner
malfunction at Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) baggage sorting facility. The scanner
was replaced in September 2024, and no further baggage incidents were reported on BLR
departures through Q4. Ms. Patel received INR 3,500 compensation and a complimentary
lounge voucher, and her feedback in the Q4 survey indicated improved satisfaction.

Fleet Planning Outlook
------------------------

IndiGo's firm order book includes 12 additional A321neo and 5 ATR 72-600 deliveries
through FY 2025-26. The A321neo additions will primarily serve international expansion on
routes from DEL and BOM to Southeast Asian destinations, while the ATR fleet will strengthen
regional connectivity from HYD, BLR, and the newly operational Mopa International Airport
(GOX) in Goa — expected to relieve congestion pressure on the legacy Dabolim Airport (GOI)
that contributed to diversions during the monsoon season.

Maintenance capacity expansion is underway with a new dedicated IndiGo MRO facility at
Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD), scheduled for operational readiness in Q3 2025.
This facility will handle C-Checks for both A320 and ATR types, reducing dependence on
Air India Engineering at DEL and third-party MROs in Singapore.
