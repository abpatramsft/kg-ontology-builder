IndiGo Airlines — Operational Performance & Delay Analysis Report (Q4 2024)
===========================================================================

Compiled by: Network Operations Centre (NOC), IndiGo Airlines
Review Period: October – December 2024
Classification: Internal Use Only

Route Performance & On-Time Metrics
-------------------------------------

The Delhi (DEL) to Mumbai (BOM) corridor remained the highest-frequency domestic route
with 49 weekly departures on flight 6E-201, operating primarily on A320 aircraft (AC001,
registration VT-INA). On-time performance for this route averaged 82.3% for the quarter,
with most delays concentrated in the November fog season. ATC-imposed ground delays at
Indira Gandhi International Airport contributed 65% of total delay minutes on DEL departures.

The Bangalore (BLR) to Hyderabad (HYD) sector, served by route R005, showed the best
punctuality in the network at 96.1% OTP. Flight 6E-205 operating this route on aircraft
AC005 (VT-INE) recorded zero cancellations over the quarter. The short 545 km distance
and reliable weather patterns at both Kempegowda International Airport and Rajiv Gandhi
International Airport contributed to this performance.

International routes showed mixed results. The Delhi to Dubai (DXB) service via route R009
experienced three significant delays in December due to runway maintenance at Dubai
International Airport. Flight F009 accumulated 145 delay minutes across these events, though
the Airbus A321neo aircraft AC002 (VT-INB) operated without any mechanical dispatch issues.

Crew Scheduling & Fatigue Management
--------------------------------------

Pilot-in-Command Capt. Anil Kapoor (CR001, employee EMP10001) completed 247 block hours
during Q4, well within the DGCA-mandated 1000-hour annual limit. Based at DEL with 22 years
of experience and an ATPL qualification, Capt. Kapoor primarily commanded DEL-BOM and DEL-BLR
sectors. No fatigue reports were filed.

First Officer Priya Nair (CR002, EMP10002) logged 312 block hours across 98 sectors,
predominantly on the BOM-GOI and BOM-BLR routes. Her CPL qualification and 8 years of
experience place her on the promotion track for command upgrade evaluation in Q2 2025.

Senior cabin crew member Rahul Verma (CR006, EMP10006) flagged a roster concern — three
consecutive red-eye flights (F010, F012, F014) assigned within a 5-day window in November.
The crew rostering team adjusted subsequent assignments to ensure compliant rest periods.
The base at Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (BOM) was identified as needing
two additional cabin crew members to maintain adequate reserve staffing levels.

Passenger Booking Trends
--------------------------

SuperSaver fares accounted for 52% of all bookings in Q4, with an average ticket price
of INR 4,200 on domestic routes. Business Plus bookings saw a 14% increase compared to Q3,
driven largely by corporate travel on the DEL-BOM and BLR-BOM corridors. Passenger Aarav
Sharma (P001, Platinum tier, frequent flyer FF100001) flew 14 sectors during the quarter,
predominantly in Flexi class. His feedback highlighted satisfaction with web check-in and
meal selection options.

Passenger Diya Patel (P002, Gold tier) raised a complaint regarding baggage handling on
flight F003 from BLR to DEL on 2024-01-15 — her checked baggage (15 kg) was delayed by
6 hours at DEL arrivals. The incident coincided with an ATC slot delay (incident I001) that
caused ground handling resource reallocation at the Delhi hub. Compensation of INR 2,500
was processed under the passenger charter.

Web check-in adoption reached 71% across all bookings, up from 63% in Q3. Passengers booking
through the 6E app showed 89% web check-in rates versus 54% for those booking via third-party
OTAs. Veg meal preference dominated at 58% of pre-orders, followed by Non-Veg at 34%.
