IndiGo Airlines — Aircraft Maintenance & Incident Summary (Jan 2024)
=====================================================================

Prepared by: Engineering & Maintenance Division, IndiGo Airlines
Report Month: January 2024
Classification: Internal Use Only

Fleet Maintenance Overview
---------------------------

A total of 20 maintenance events were logged across the IndiGo fleet during January 2024.
The most significant activity was a C-Check on aircraft AC011 (registration VT-INK, Airbus
A320-200 variant) conducted at the Air India Engineering facility at DEL. The inspection,
tagged as maintenance record M001, ran from January 10 to January 25 with an estimated cost
of INR 45,00,000. During the inspection, a crack was found in the nose gear door bracket,
requiring immediate repair. The aircraft was returned to active status by January 28.

Aircraft AC015 (VT-INO, ATR 72-600) underwent a scheduled A-Check (M005) at IndiGo
Hangar-HYD from January 12 to January 14. The inspection cost INR 3,50,000 and revealed
worn brake pads requiring replacement. The next scheduled maintenance for this aircraft
falls on April 14, 2024. The ATR fleet based at smaller airports like Rajiv Gandhi
International (A005, HYD) continues to show lower maintenance costs compared to the
narrow-body Airbus fleet, averaging INR 4,20,000 per event versus INR 28,00,000.

Engine-related maintenance was performed on AC003 (VT-INC, A321neo) under maintenance
record M003 — a hot section inspection at Pratt & Whitney MRO-SIN facility. Technician
TEC003 supervised the work, which ran from January 5 to January 20 at a cost of INR
65,00,000. No critical findings were reported, and the aircraft is next due for inspection
on July 20, 2024.

Incident Report Summary
-------------------------

Fifteen incidents were recorded in January 2024 across the operational fleet. The majority
were classified as Low severity, reflecting the airline's strong safety culture.

Incident I001 involved flight F003 on aircraft AC003 on January 15. An ATC slot delay at
DEL resulted in a 30-minute departure delay during the ground phase. The incident was
classified Low severity, closed without DGCA reporting, and attributed to airspace
congestion over northern India — a recurring issue during peak morning departure banks
at Indira Gandhi International Airport.

Incident I003, also on January 15, was a Medium-severity bird strike on aircraft AC005
(VT-INE) during the approach phase of flight F005. The bird struck the radome area, and
a post-flight inspection revealed minor composite damage. The aircraft was grounded for
a 2-day inspection before being cleared for service. This incident was reported to DGCA
as required under CAR Section 5, Series F, Part I.

The most operationally significant incident was I005 — a hydraulic leak warning on aircraft
AC010 (VT-INJ, A320-200) during cruise phase on flight F010 from DEL to GOI via route R005.
The crew executed the hydraulic system abnormal checklist, and the aircraft landed normally
at Goa (Dabolim Airport, A006, GOI). Maintenance found a faulty hydraulic line connector,
which was replaced on-station. The incident was classified Medium severity and reported to
DGCA. Aircraft AC010 returned to service within 48 hours after hydraulic system pressure
tests confirmed nominal performance.

No High-severity incidents were recorded during the month. The airline's incident rate of
0.5 per 1,000 flight hours remains well below the industry average of 1.2 per 1,000 hours
for carriers of comparable fleet size operating in the South Asian region.
