IndiGo Airlines — Crew Training & Compliance Report (H1 2024)
================================================================

Prepared by: Flight Safety & Training Department, IndiGo Airlines
Review Period: January – June 2024
Classification: Internal Use Only

Recurrent Training Overview
-----------------------------

A total of 34 crew training events were conducted during the first half of 2024 across
IndiGo's three primary training centres: the Full Flight Simulator Complex at DEL (TC-DEL),
the Cabin Emergency Trainer facility at BOM (TC-BOM), and the Computer-Based Training hub
at BLR (TC-BLR).

Captain Anil Kapoor (CR001, EMP10001) completed his biannual Line Proficiency Check (LPC)
on the A320 type rating on March 12, 2024 at TC-DEL. His simulator session included an
engine-out approach into Goa (GOI, Dabolim Airport) replicating the crosswind conditions
documented in flight F010's hydraulic incident (I005, January 15). The training examiner
noted excellent CRM and decision-making under asymmetric thrust conditions. Capt. Kapoor's
next LPC is due September 2024.

First Officer Priya Nair (CR002, EMP10002) underwent her Command Upgrade Assessment (CUA)
preparatory module in April 2024. The four-day programme at TC-DEL covered V1 cut
procedures, GPWS escape manoeuvres, and non-precision approaches into terrain-challenging
airports such as Rajiv Gandhi International (HYD) and Cochin International (COK). Her
performance was rated "Above Standard" — she is now cleared for the formal command upgrade
evaluation scheduled in Q2 2025 as noted by Network Operations.

Senior Cabin Crew Rahul Verma (CR006, EMP10006) completed the annual Safety Equipment &
Procedure (SEP) recurrent training at TC-BOM in February 2024. The session included live
raft deployment, fire suppression drill, and a scenario based on the bird strike incident
I003 (January 15, AC005/VT-INE) where cabin crew must manage passenger anxiety during an
unplanned diversion. Verma scored 94% on the written assessment and received special
commendation for leadership during the evacuation drill.

Incident-Triggered Training
------------------------------

Following the hydraulic leak incident I005 on aircraft AC010 (VT-INJ), an Operational
Safety Bulletin (OSB-2024-007) was issued on February 1, 2024. All A320 pilots received a
mandatory 2-hour eLearning module covering hydraulic system architecture, failure mode
identification, and the revised Hydraulic System Abnormal checklist. Completion was tracked
via the Learning Management System (LMS-6E), and 100% compliance was achieved by February
28, 2024.

The bird strike incident I003 prompted a refresher module on Bird Strike Avoidance and
Post-Impact Procedures for all DEL and BLR-based crews. The module referenced AC005's radome
damage and the subsequent 2-day grounding, emphasising the importance of accurate pilot
reports for maintenance decision-making. Training record TR-2024-019 confirms 98% completion
among the target crew population, with two members (both on medical leave) scheduled for
make-up sessions in March.

Type Rating & Fleet Transition
-------------------------------

With the planned induction of three additional ATR 72-600 aircraft by Q3 2024, a batch of
eight pilots commenced ATR type-rating conversion at the ATR Training Centre in Toulouse
(TC-TLS) in May 2024. The cohort includes four pilots transitioning from the A320 fleet,
requiring a 6-week bridge course covering differences in fly-by-wire vs. conventional
controls, propeller handling, and short-field operations typical of IndiGo's regional
network serving airports like HYD and Vizag (VTZ).

Technician Certification
--------------------------

Maintenance technician TEC003, who supervised the engine hot section inspection on AC003
(VT-INC, M003) at Pratt & Whitney MRO-SIN, completed an advanced PW1100G-JM engine module
course in April 2024. This certification enables TEC003 to independently sign off on borescope
inspections and turbine blade replacements without MRO facility supervision, reducing future
turnaround times for engine events on the A321neo sub-fleet.

Technician TEC007 (assigned to IndiGo Hangar-HYD) attained certification on the ATR 72-600
PW127M powerplant after completing the 120-hour practical module. This directly supports
the planned A-Check schedule for aircraft AC015 (VT-INO), whose next inspection falls on
April 14, 2024 as noted in the maintenance log M005.

DGCA Audit Readiness
-----------------------

IndiGo's training compliance score, as measured against DGCA CAR Section 7 Series M Part I,
stood at 99.2% at the end of June 2024. The only gap identified was a 3-day overdue
Dangerous Goods Awareness refresher for two ground handling staff at Kempegowda
International Airport (BLR). Corrective action was completed by June 18, and the finding
was downgraded from "Level 2" to "Observation" during the DGCA surveillance audit on
June 25, 2024.

The Safety Management System (SMS) team flagged 12 Voluntary Safety Reports (VSR) during
H1 2024. Three reports were directly linked to ATC congestion at DEL — corroborating the
trend documented in incident I001 (ATC slot delays during peak morning banks at Indira
Gandhi International Airport). These reports informed a proposal to adjust the DEL departure
schedule by shifting two early-morning flights (including 6E-201 on the DEL-BOM route) by
15 minutes to reduce slot conflict probability.
