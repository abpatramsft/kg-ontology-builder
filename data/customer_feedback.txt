IndiGo Airlines — Customer Feedback & Cabin Quality Report (Q3 2025)
=====================================================================

Compiled by: Passenger Experience Division, IndiGo Airlines (InterGlobe Aviation Ltd.)
Review Period: July – September 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

Cabin Comfort & Seating — A320neo Fleet
----------------------------------------

Multiple complaints received regarding seat recline mechanism failures on A320neo
aircraft VT-ITB and VT-ITC during July 2025. Passengers on routes 6E-2041 (DEL-BOM)
and 6E-5523 (BLR-CCU) reported jammed recline levers in rows 14-18. Root cause
traced to a batch of faulty Recaro CL3710 slimline seats installed during the April
2025 cabin refresh cycle. Recaro Aviation issued a service bulletin (SB-CL3710-042)
recommending actuator arm replacement. 47 seats across 3 aircraft were remediated
by end of August. Passenger satisfaction for seat comfort improved from 3.1 to 3.8
(out of 5.0) after the fix.

In-seat USB charging ports manufactured by Astronics showed a 12% failure rate on
the A321neo fleet, particularly on aircraft VT-ITE and VT-ITF. Astronics shipped
replacement PEC Series 2 EmPower units in August. All units replaced by September 15.
Charging port complaint rate dropped from 8.2 per 1000 passengers to 1.1 per 1000.

In-Flight Entertainment & Connectivity
---------------------------------------

The Panasonic Avionics eX3 IFE system on A321neo aircraft experienced intermittent
screen blackouts during monsoon-season turbulence. Engineering analysis by Panasonic
Avionics Corporation indicated loose ribbon cable connectors at the seatback display
mount. A revised connector bracket (PN: PAC-EX3-BKT-07) was approved for retrofit.
12 aircraft scheduled for retrofit during the October 2025 C-check cycle.

Wi-Fi connectivity powered by Thales FlytLIVE showed 94.2% uptime across the A320neo
fleet in Q3, up from 88.7% in Q2. However, passengers on ATR 72-600 regional routes
reported zero connectivity — Wi-Fi installation on the ATR fleet remains pending
budget approval. Customer NPS for connectivity on A320neo routes reached +31, while
ATR routes scored -14 due to the absence of any in-flight connectivity option.

Lavatory & Galley Systems
--------------------------

Lavatory smoke detector false alarms increased 40% quarter-over-quarter on the A320neo
fleet. Collins Aerospace, the smoke detector supplier, identified a firmware bug in the
Model 965-0026-003 detector that caused false triggers under high humidity conditions
common during monsoon operations. Firmware patch v2.4.1 was deployed fleet-wide by
August 20, 2025. False alarm rate returned to baseline levels within two weeks.

Galley chiller units from Safran Cabin (formerly Zodiac Aerospace) on VT-ITA and VT-ITD
showed compressor cycling issues leading to food temperature exceedances. Three catering
complaints from business passengers on the DEL-BOM trunk route were escalated. Safran
Cabin dispatched field service engineers who replaced the compressor control boards
(PN: SCI-GCU-1140) on both aircraft. Mean time between failures (MTBF) for the galley
chiller improved from 620 hours to projected 1,800 hours post-repair.

Cabin Air Quality & Pressurization Feedback
---------------------------------------------

Nine passenger complaints regarding dry cabin air were logged on A321neo aircraft
operating the high-altitude BLR-DEL route during August. The Honeywell 131-9A APU
bleed air system was inspected and found operating within normal parameters. However,
humidity levels measured at 4-6% RH at cruise altitude — below the 8% target recommended
by Honeywell for the HGT1700 Environmental Control System. Engineering proposed adding
Liebherr humidification modules during the next D-check cycle for VT-ITE and VT-ITF.

Pressurization anomalies were reported on ATR 72-600 VT-IQA during the Kolkata-Agartala
sector. The Hamilton Sundstrand cabin pressure controller showed erratic behavior during
descent, causing passenger ear discomfort complaints. The controller was bench-tested,
recalibrated, and returned to service with updated firmware. No recurrence reported in
September operations.

Ground Experience & Boarding
-----------------------------

Boarding process delays at Delhi T1 terminal impacted 23% of IndiGo departures in July,
averaging 18 minutes delay. Primary causes: biometric boarding gate integration issues
with the DigiYatra platform and staffing shortages at the manual document verification
counters. After adding two additional DigiYatra e-gates and cross-training ground crew,
August on-time boarding improved to 91%.

Passenger wheelchair assistance satisfaction at Mumbai CSMIA terminal scored 4.6/5.0,
the highest among Indian carriers per the DGCA Q3 accessibility audit. IndiGo's
partnership with ground handler Celebi Aviation Services for assisted passenger management
was cited as a best practice.
